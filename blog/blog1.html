Growing through two sides of a coin

DATE: 21st Sept, 2022

As you know, I am currently pursuing my Masters in Computer and Information Science at University of Michigan, Dearborn Campus. Initially when I first planned to pursue a Master's degree, I thought of doing a Ph.D. I enjoyed research, and therefore wanted to do some work in Recommendation Systems. Thereby believing that pursuing Research and doing a Ph.D. would be a right choice for me.

In part, I still find the idea of doing a Ph.D. good but the thing I need to answer is at what costs? How much time am I willing to give to pursue that and what I am willing to loose. This is something that I have been thinking about a lot in the past couple of months. At the end, its all about ROI, for me.

While pursuing a Ph.D. my main motive would be to gather information, understand and learn as much as possible about recommender systems but a MAJOR caveat would be losing my time and money. Ph.D. while studying earn a lot less say, 2K per month. I will only be able to make my living with hardly anything left to save as an investment. Even after completing the Ph.D. I would still end up at a similar job role that too at beginner position. So what am I loosing? Money. Not a little bit of it, but a lot of it. 5 years worth of it.

Now coming to another point, even if I do a Ph.D. where would I love to work? at a company that has good reach, what better than Amazon? Furthermore, it's not like I don't like to code or something, actually I love to code and I identify myself as a Coder first and then Researcher. Having said that working at Amazon, I'll be doing just that! CODE. Furthermore, I am able to do what I love, work in an area closely related to recommendation, work with world class talent no lackluster than the ones found at top universities and a cherry on the cake, I will be earning while doing all this.

I know this might be coming off a little like I am focusing too much on money, I understand your point of view. Here is the thing, what can I not do once I have money? If I have money rather I can do whatever I want, research in whatever area I want without having to answer to anyone, any organization or anything. Pursue whatever I want. See, money is an important thing, you need to know how to use it. In these days I understood how valuable it is and how you can hire talents with it, how you can utilize that to get your job done and how it makes you free to pursue what you want to at the end. The only thing that you need is a lot of money.

So thinking back, is pursuing a Ph.D. worth it? just for the experience of getting to learn from world renowned professors? NOPE. Not for me. Because for me, my time is much more valuable. I can learn whatever I want to by myself, I evaluate myself as someone who is good at self learning. I love to code. I love to work with talents, which there are a lot where I work. AND I love to be valued

The only thing a Ph.D. provides is some degree of freedom for the short term. I am not here for the short term benefit, but for the longer play. Longer play as in, working for myself. Not for the university, not for the company. Maybe I will start my own university in India? who knows. But that's what I mean by longer play.

My current plan is along these lines, which is open to changes. Firstly I will be atleast working for 10-14yrs, then I will be starting something helpful, big and while living in India, inventing something exciting. I don't have any clue about what I will be focusing on but I know, I will work on something or the other for myself! Something like this will probably not be possible if I go into Academia from the beginning. Because, out of those 15years, 5 years would already go for the Ph.D.

Also, Just a side note which is rather obvious, these are my thoughts alone. It might not be for everyone, just some musings. Ph.D. is a good option if you want to go to Academia, if you really really love research or if you need a break from industry. It was just not for me.

Whatever you choose, make sure to experience both sides of it. Also I learned a lot from my Research Assistantship, which I will be writing in some blog post soon, so be on a lookout for it!

-Sarvesh Bhatnagar
