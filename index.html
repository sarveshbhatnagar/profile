01 — About

A bit about me

I'm Sarvesh Bhatnagar — an engineer and researcher driven by curiosity. Currently working as an SDE at Amazon, where I build distributed systems spanning ML Infrastructure, LLM evaluation pipelines, notification services, and authorization frameworks.

My research sits at the intersection of serverless computing and systems design. At the University of Michigan, I worked on open-source serverless search — exploring how functions are discovered and composed across ecosystems. I'm interested in how ML infrastructure and serverless primitives reshape what we can build at scale.

Outside of work I write at introtoalgo.com, exploring algorithms, data structures, and computing ideas in an accessible way. I believe in consistent learning, confident humility, and grounded self-reflection.

04 — Research

Publications

IEEE PerCom 2023 · *LESS Workshop

OS3: The Art and the Practice of Searching for Open-Source Serverless Functions

Presents OS3, a search approach that maximises utility of serverless function results by combining keyword and library-usage matching, filtering by software licence. Built over a corpus of 5,981 serverless repositories from AWS, Azure, IBM Cloud, and the Serverless Framework.
